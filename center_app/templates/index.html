About Us:

"The Royal Project" was founded in the summer of 2020 by Yosef Daniel and Jezliah Villarreal. Its headquarters is located in the heart of Ramat Beit Shemesh, Israel. The idea of the name "Royal" comes from the meaning of their last name, Villarreal, which means "Royal Palace". This is their vision, their project...

Under the Artistic Direction of Jezliah, "The Royal Project" Center for Music and Dance will bring the community of Beit Shemesh a one-of-a-kind program that focuses on strengthening the artistic talent of each individual while helping them build self confidence, self-esteem and pride in their Jewish heritage. It will also provide a platform in which it will inspire engagement through performance opportunities.

Music and dance have become a trendy art form creating a platform for all ages all over the world. Within our community, as religious Jews, we see the influence of this art form. The Center is not only a "chug" but a vision and place that provides Jewish girls and boys, women and men, professional training in the performing arts. Our experience for the past 25 years has prepared us to provide this generation an outlet of expression through music and dance, in a modern and revolutionary approach all within Torah observance. Our instructors are the best in skill and performance and are eager to inspire the future dancers, singers and musicians of tomorrow!

The RPCMD uses only the best of Jewish and instrumental music and maintains a strong focus on tzniut in classes and performances.
Please note: Our Girls/Women Division performs only for women audiences.

We all have a special and unique spark that Hashem has instilled within us to bring more good into this world.
Our mission is to...
CREATE, INSPIRE and IGNITE!

Jezliah Villarreal, Director:   Being the daughter of a christian evangelical pastor, Jezliah grew up in a religious environment, away from the secular world. At a young age, Jezliah found a love for the biblical stories and teachings and more so the music and songs in the Christian world. The performing arts became her connection to G-d and her way of life. Jezliah began dancing at the age of 8 when she was introduced to Lyrical, an expressive form of dance. She started a dance group of tambourine players at the age of 9, teaching and leading a group of mostly teens and women. Along with dance, Jezliah found her love of singing and at the age of 13 became the youngest member of the church's "worship band". Seeing a need for the performing arts within the church, Jezliah opened a department for the arts. At the age of 15, not only was she directing a group of dancers from various ages but she took center as the leader of the worship band, produced plays and organized events within the community and other churches. Jezliah continued her dance training throughout her high school years and was eager to join the dance team but many of the performances didn't allow her to attend as they were all on Shabbat. This pushed Jezliah to keep creating and strengthening the performing arts department at her church. To read about Jezliah and her family's journey to Judaism click here.

Jezliah is an inspirational Performer, Singer and Teacher and is continuing her work of inspiring women and girls in the Jewish community through the performing arts. Her performances have led to speaking engagements throughout her city and all over Israel about her incredible journey to Judaism. Her creativeness and successful reputation has caught the attention of local producers and the Event Coordinators of the Aliyah Absorption Department and City Municipality.

She is the Co-Founder and former Creative Co-Director and Teacher at the RBS Dance and Music Academy located in Ramat Beit Shemesh from 2011-2019. She has trained over 500 women and girls of different hashkafas and has brought about unity and self development throughout the community of Beit Shemesh. Jezliah focuses on a modern and purposeful training method that attracts women and girls in the orthodox community with her fun and wholesome personality and energy.

She has co-produced many productions as former director of the RBSDMA and has taught workshops in Beit Shemesh and Jerusalem. She has performed as a Singing Artist for women events hosted by the Dames of the Dance in Gush Etzion, Beit Shemesh Aliyah Absorption Department in collaboration with Daniel Aloni Studios, and Spotlight on Women.

In March 2018, Jezliah produced a successful variety show for over 300 women and girls for International Women's Day hosted by the Aliyah Department of Beit Shemesh. In November 2018, she also co-produced a concert featuring Artists Yonatan Razel, Ari Goldwag and her husband, Yosef Daniel.

Jezliah is currently working on her first album and the grand opening of The Royal Project Center for Music and Dance. She observes Kol Isha and performs only for women and girls. Jezliah's incredible story has been heard by hundreds of women, schools and organizations such as Bnos Hashraah. Jezliah hopes to bring a powerful program that will enable all to reach their full potential, no matter where they come from.

Yosef Daniel Villarreal:   Born 1981 in Reynosa, Mexico, raised in Texas, Yosef Daniel had a keen inclination for the traditional Spanish Guitar at an early age. YD started his musical career as a guitarist at the Del Mar College summer camp in 1995 in Corpus Christi, Texas. He started composing and writing songs on his guitar and he performed for his family, friends and classmates.

His love for performing led him to pursue a career in music, landing him on platforms and stages across the US, Mexico and recently Israel. YD formed part of several bands before embarking on his own as a solo artist. YD toured as a guitarist and singer and appeared as a featured guitarist for english and spanish bands.

YD has served his community in Israel for ten years teaching private guitar and drum lessons. His recent solo productions have captured the radio airwaves and music charts in New York, New Jersey, Mexico and Israel. Israeli radio such as Gal Galatz and Radio Kol Chai have added YD's songs to their playlists.

YD has over ten years experience and training serving as counselor and mentor for youth camps across America. Throughout his musical production YD has taken part in musicals, plays, theater and dance. YD has a unique story that has captured audiences of all ages in Israel and abroad. His unique approach and outlook on life as a jewish artist is held valuable within the community.
